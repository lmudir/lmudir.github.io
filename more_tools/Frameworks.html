Security Frameworks

An overview of common security frameworks and how they relate to SOC work.

Why frameworks matter

Frameworks provide a shared language and structure to manage cybersecurity risk across people, processes and technology. For SOC analysts and penetration testers they help prioritise detection, response and controls, and provide context for reporting.

They are organizational: useful for aligning work and measuring progress, not step‑by‑step instructions for a single analyst.

Short comparisons

NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)

High‑level, function‑based: Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover. Useful to map program goals and maturity; flexible and widely adopted.

ISO 27001

Certifiable Information Security Management System (ISMS). Focuses on governance, documented risk assessments, controls and continuous improvement; often used where formal certification is required.

NIST 800‑61 (Incident Response)

Practical guidance for incident response lifecycle and playbooks. Good source for structuring response steps and tabletop exercises.

ENS (Esquema Nacional de Seguridad)

Spanish national security requirements for public sector and critical services. Similar compliance/governance focus but regionally specific.

MITRE ATT&CK

An adversary behaviour matrix used to map observed techniques and tactics to detection and response actions. Highly practical for SOC detection engineering and case documentation.

How these fit together in practice

  • NIST CSF defines program functions and outcomes (what to achieve).
  • ISO 27001 formalises policies, roles and continuous improvement where certification or formal governance is needed.
  • NIST 800‑61 provides structure for incident response playbooks and exercises.
  • MITRE ATT&CK maps attacker techniques to detections and helps prioritise coverage gaps.

Practical pattern: map a SIEM alert to an ATT&CK technique, document detection and containment steps in a playbook (NIST 800‑61 style), and tag the activity under CSF Detect/Respond functions for program reporting.
